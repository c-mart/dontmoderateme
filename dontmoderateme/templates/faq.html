Frequently Asked Questions

{% endblock %} {% block body %}

Feel free to contact us if your question is not answered below.

What does it cost?
Don't Moderate Me is a free service. Someday there may be paid options that add more functionality, but what you see now will remain free.

How many pages can I monitor?
Right now DMM allows each user to monitor 60 pages. This will change as the service grows and scales in capacity.

How quickly do I get notified?
DMM checks each monitor at least once each day, so you should get notified within 24 hours. These checks will become more frequent in the future.

Why do emails from DMM end up in my spam folder?
Don't Moderate Me is a new service and we're still working to earn the trust of major email providers' spam filters. If you use Gmail and find our messages in your spam folder, push the "Not Spam" button. If you continue to have this problem, add notify@dontmoderate.me to your safe/trusted senders list, or dontmoderate.me to your safe/trusted domains list, as applicable. (The Gmail equivalent of this is creating a contact for notify@dontmoderate.me.)

Why doesn't it work for private sites (like Facebook)?
Don't Moderate Me cannot view private or restricted content that requires a login. DMM doesn't know how to log into Facebook as you - we don't want to collect your Facebook password! This fits with the vision of DMM as a tool to provide transparency and accountability for public discussions.

How secure is Don't Moderate Me?
I am implementing best practices that enhance the security of Don't Moderate Me, but work remains to be done. Like any web-based service, DMM could be hacked or breached by a malicious party. Right now, DMM is a proof-of-concept that has never received an independent security assessment. Until that changes, please don't use DMM to monitor content that you cannot ever have publicly associated with the email or IP address that you use to access the service.

What is your privacy policy?
Aside from the security considerations above, each DMM user is considered confidential, including their monitors and usage habits. The personally identifiable information that you provide to DMM will never be disclosed to a third party (to the extent permitted by law), only ever used to troubleshoot problems and improve the service.
